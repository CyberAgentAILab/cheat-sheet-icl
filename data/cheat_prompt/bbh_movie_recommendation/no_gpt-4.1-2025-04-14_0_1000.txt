**CHEAT SHEET FOR "FIND A SIMILAR MOVIE" QUESTIONS**

---

### 1. **Recognize the Pattern:**
- The correct answer is almost always a **famous, critically acclaimed, or blockbuster film** from the **same era/genre** as the examples.
- The answer is **never an obscure, niche, or obviously unrelated film**.
- If the options include a movie that is as famous as the examples, it's likely the answer.

---

### 2. **Commonly Repeated "Anchor" Movies and Their Clusters:**

#### **A. 90s Blockbusters/Prestige Dramas Cluster**
- **Examples:** Forrest Gump, The Shawshank Redemption, Pulp Fiction, Braveheart, Schindler's List, The Fugitive, Dances with Wolves, Apollo 13, The Usual Suspects, Saving Private Ryan, Goodfellas, LA Confidential, Fargo, The Silence of the Lambs, Jurassic Park, Independence Day, Toy Story, Batman, The Lion King, Aladdin, Pretty Woman, The Mask, Terminator 2: Judgment Day, Inception, The Matrix, Raiders of the Lost Ark, Star Wars (original trilogy), Gladiator, Gattaca, Interview with the Vampire, Mr. Holland's Opus, Get Shorty, Don Juan DeMarco, Tombstone, Stargate, Dead Man Walking, Crimson Tide, Heat, Philadelphia, American Beauty, Back to the Future, Inside Out, Edge of Tomorrow, Monsters, Inc, Interstellar, Star Wars Episode VII, The Lord of the Rings (Fellowship, Two Towers, Return of the King).

- **If the question lists 3+ of these, the answer is almost always another from this group.**

#### **B. Animated/Family Blockbusters**
- **Examples:** The Lion King, Toy Story, Aladdin, Beauty and the Beast, The Jungle Book, Willy Wonka, The Wizard of Oz, Pinocchio, Snow White, The Secret Garden.
- **If the question lists 3+ of these, the answer is another classic family/animated film.**

#### **C. Sci-Fi/Action Blockbusters**
- **Examples:** The Matrix, Terminator 2, Independence Day, Star Wars, The Fifth Element, Gladiator, Gattaca, Inception, Edge of Tomorrow, Back to the Future, Jurassic Park, The Terminator, Planet of the Apes, X-Men, Spider-Man, The Dark Knight.
- **If the question lists 3+ of these, the answer is another from this group.**

### 5. **If All Options Are Obscure Except One Famous Movie:**
- **Pick the famous one.**

---

### 6. **If the Question Mixes Genres (e.g., Animation, Action, Drama):**
- **Pick the most "blockbuster" or "prestige" film in the options.**

---

### 7. **If the Question Includes a Mix of Animated and Live-Action Blockbusters:**
- **Pick the answer that is a blockbuster from the same era, even if it's a different genre.**

---

### 8. **If the Question Includes a Lot of 90s/00s Prestige Dramas/Blockbusters:**
- **Pick another 90s/00s prestige drama/blockbuster.**

---

### 9. **If the Question Includes a Lot of 80s/90s Action/Sci-Fi:**
- **Pick another 80s/90s action/sci-fi blockbuster.**

---

### 10. **If the Question Includes a Lot of Classic Family/Animated Films:**
- **Pick another classic family/animated film.**

---

### 11. **If the Question Includes a Lot of Crime/Thriller/Drama:**
- **Pick another famous crime/thriller/drama from the same era.**

---

### 12. **If the Question Includes a Lot of "Cult" or "Quirky" Films:**
- **Pick another cult/quirky film from the same era.**

---

### 13. **If Unsure, Eliminate:**
- **Eliminate any option that is:**
  - Obscure
  - Clearly a different genre/era
  - Not a blockbuster or critically acclaimed

---

**Summary:**  
**Look for the most famous, critically acclaimed, or blockbuster film in the options that matches the era/genre of the examples.**  
**If in doubt, pick the answer that is a 90s/00s blockbuster or prestige drama.**  
**If the options are all obscure except one, pick the famous one.**  
**If the question is a mix, pick the most "blockbuster" or "prestige" film.**
